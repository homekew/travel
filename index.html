01Know Before You Go — 2026

Verified July 2026. Fees changed significantly this year, so read this box before your first trip.

Passes & Fees

  • NW Forest Pass: $5/day · $30/yr — covers most Deschutes & Ochoco NF day-use sites (Cascade Lakes, Metolius, Simax Beach). America the Beautiful ($80) also works.
  • Oregon State Parks parking: $10/day resident · $12 non-res · $60/yr (doubled Jan 1, 2026). Charged at Tumalo, Smith Rock, Cove Palisades, La Pine, Prineville Reservoir.
  • Newberry NVM: $5/vehicle/day or NW Forest Pass — one pass covers Paulina/East Lake, Paulina Falls, Lava Lands.

Central Cascades Wilderness Permits

June 15 – Oct 15, 2026: day-use permits required at 19 trailheads including Green Lakes/Fall Creek, Broken Top, Tam McArthur Rim, and hikes beyond Todd Lake. $1/person on Recreation.gov, released 10 days and 2 days ahead at 7 am. Not needed to visit the drive-up lakes themselves.

New for 2026: Waterway Access Permit

As of Jan 1, 2026 all non-motorized boats need a permit regardless of length — kayaks, canoes, SUPs, rafts, inflatable kayaks. $6/wk · $20/yr. Exempt: kids 13 and under, river float tubes, surfboards. Stop at orange boat-inspection stations when carrying any watercraft.

Seasonal Roads

  • Cascade Lakes Hwy & Paulina Lake Rd opened May 7, 2026 (early); both close with November snow.
  • Three Creek Rd (FS 16) usually drivable late June–July.
  • Tumalo Falls Rd gated Nov–May (bike/walk in).
  • Alder Springs road closed Dec 1 – Mar 31 (deer winter range).

Algae (Cyanobacteria) Advisories

Repeat bloomers: Lake Billy Chinook (advisory June 12, 2026, downgraded June 29 — still risky for dogs), Prineville Reservoir, Ochoco Reservoir, Crane Prairie, Wickiup, Odell, Haystack. Check the OHA advisories page before swimming July–Oct. When in doubt, stay out; kids and dogs are highest-risk.

The Bend Float, 2026

Park & Float (1000 SW Bradbury Way): free parking, tube rentals, June 13 – Sep 7. Ride the River shuttle: $5 (cash or app), June 17 – Sep 7, SUPs now allowed space-permitting. Float Riverbend Park → Drake Park, ~1.5–2 hrs. Life jackets required for kids 12 & under on any boat/SUP.

River Flows Cheat Sheet

  • Deschutes below Wickiup/Sunriver: runs HIGH Apr–mid-Oct (irrigation releases) — swifter than it looks.
  • Middle Deschutes (below Bend): runs LOW all summer — mellower, warmer.
  • Spring-fed & steady year-round: Metolius, Fall River, Spring River — cold (~46–52°F) but crystal clear and predictable.

Fire & Closure Status

  • Deschutes NF Stage 1 fire restrictions May 18 – Nov 30, 2026.
  • 2024 Little Lava Fire burn scars visible near Lava Lakes/Wickiup; emergency closures lifted, watch for snags.
  • McKenzie River Trail: partial detour (Lookout Fire); Cedar Creek closure order through 2026 farther west.
  • Walton Lake: ~⅓ mile of shore trail closed (hazard trees); lake open.
  • Live info: centraloregonfire.org

05Suggested Itineraries

Field-tested combinations. Times assume a relaxed family pace with snack stops.

1 · The Volcano Water Day (South)

Full day · 55 min drive · Newberry NVM pass

Paulina Falls viewpoint (30 min) → Little Crater CG, hike 1.2 mi of lakeshore to Paulina Lake Hot Springs, dig a soaking pool, cold-plunge the lake (2–3 hrs) → picnic → drive to East Lake pumice beach for the warm afternoon swim (2 hrs) → Big Obsidian Flow trail on the way out (45 min). Teens rate this the best single day in the guide.

2 · The Crystal River Day (Northwest)

Full day · 50–60 min · $5 day pass

Head of the Metolius (20 min wonder-stop) → Camp Sherman Bridge trout-spotting + store ice cream → Wizard Falls Hatchery: feed the fish, picnic on the lawn → walk the West Metolius Trail 1 mile to the gushing springs → optional finish: Suttle Lake swim + lodge milkshake on the drive home.

3 · Cascade Lakes Sampler (West)

Full day · 40–55 min · NW Forest Pass

Todd Lake morning loop + frog hunt (90 min) → Sparks Lake Ray Atkeson lava trail + photos (1 hr) → Devils Lake turquoise polar-plunge dare (30 min) → afternoon at Elk Lake beach or a Hosmer Lake canoe safari. Pack layers — it's 10° cooler up here.

4 · Waterfall & Rowboat Day (Northwest, Hwy 126)

Full day · 70 min · free–$5

Sahalie & Koosah Falls 0.8-mi loop in the cool morning mist → drive 5 min to Clear Lake: rent a rowboat and drift over the 3,000-year-old sunken forest → cafe lunch → optional Tamolitch Blue Pool hike (3.7 mi RT) for families with strong hikers — view only, no jumping, ever.

5 · The Lazy Float Day (Bend)

Half day · 10 min · $5 shuttle + rentals

Park at Park & Float, rent tubes → put in at Riverbend Park → float to the Whitewater Park: brave kids ride the Passageway Channel, everyone else walks around at McKay Park → continue to Drake Park take-out → shuttle back → burgers in the Old Mill and watch the surfers from the footbridge.

6 · Desert Canyon Adventure (North)

Full day · 40–50 min · start EARLY in summer

Morning hike to Steelhead Falls (0.5 mi) — swim the big pool, older kids explore downstream beaches (3 hrs) → picnic → afternoon at Tumalo State Park for easy swimming, or Smith Rock river-trail wade in shoulder season. Rattlesnake talk beforehand; gallons of water.

7 · Waterslide & Big Tree Day (South)

Full day · 40 min · mostly free

McKay Crossing: hike 1.5 mi up Paulina Creek to the natural rock waterslide (wear old shorts!) + falls swimming hole (3 hrs) → La Pine State Park: swim beach, then the 500-year-old Big Tree → finish wading the warm Little Deschutes at Rosland Park.

8 · The Beach Commitment (South)

Full day · 80 min · $5 pass

Drive early to Crescent Lake's Simax Beach — claim sand, swim the endless shallow shelf, build the castle, paddle the protected bay (all day) → detour on the way home: Odell Lake's Trapper Creek in fall for spawning kokanee, or Davis Lake's lava dam scramble in early summer.

9 · Alpine Earn-Your-Swim (West/NW, teens)

Full day · 55–65 min · wilderness permit may apply

Drive up FS 16 to Three Creek Lake → hike 1.5–2.5 mi to Little Three Creek Lake for the warmer, emptier swim under Tam McArthur Rim → back to the big lake for a rowboat hour. Alternative: Six Lakes Trail to Blow & Doris Lakes ($1 wilderness permit, book 2–10 days out).

06Family Water Safety Code

The six rules that cover 95% of the risk out here:

1 · Cold is the hidden hazard

Even in August most water here runs 45–65°F. Cold shock — involuntary gasping and instant fatigue — is the region's top drowning factor, not current. Swim parallel to shore, keep sessions short, warm up between dips. Spring-fed waters (Metolius, Fall River, Devils Lake, Blue Pool at 37°F) never warm up.

2 · Know your take-out

The Deschutes alternates flat water with killer falls. Absolute exit points: Drake Park (town float), Wyeth CG (above Pringle Falls), Sunriver takeout / Benham East (above Benham Falls), upstream of the log boom at Dillon Falls. Brief the kids before launching, every time.

3 · No cliff jumping

Deaths and serious injuries recur at Tamolitch Blue Pool (most recently June 2026) and Steelhead Falls. Depths change yearly, rocks move, cold water incapacitates, and rescue is hours away. Make the family rule explicit before you arrive, not at the edge.

4 · Check algae before reservoir swims

July–October, check OHA cyanobacteria advisories for Billy Chinook, Prineville, Ochoco, Crane Prairie, Wickiup, Odell, Haystack. Blooms look like green paint or pea soup. Dogs are most vulnerable.

5 · Life jackets & the law

Required for kids 12 and under on any boat, raft, or SUP, and smart for everyone in moving water. Afternoon wind builds real waves on Elk, Cultus, Crescent, Odell, and Big Lake — paddle mornings, hug the shore.

6 · Canyon logistics

Steelhead Falls, Alder Springs, Scout Camp, Chimney Rock: no cell service, full sun, rattlesnake country. One liter of water per person minimum, water shoes for fords, and tell someone your plan. Everywhere past Mt. Bachelor: assume zero bars.
